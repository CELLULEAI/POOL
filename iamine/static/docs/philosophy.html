Why ≥9B: our quality floor

Cellule.ai is a heterogeneous network. Any capable machine can join and contribute compute — a laptop, a workstation, a GPU rig. That openness is the whole point. But openness to participation is not the same as lowering the bar on answers. We hold a deliberate quality floor: the network does not serve a final answer from a model below 9B-class quality. Here is why — and why your small machine still matters.

Two things that look the same but aren't

There are two distinct questions in a distributed inference network, and conflating them is how quality quietly rots:

QuestionOur answer
Who can participate?Any capable machine. From a laptop to a GPU rig. Heterogeneous atoms are the network's strength, not a compromise.
What is good enough to answer?9B-class and up. Below that, by experience, the answer is not reliable enough to ship to a user.

What experience taught us

We started open to the romantic ideal: a swarm of tiny models, each doing a little. In practice, a 1B model asked 17 × 23 confidently returns 401. A 3B drifts on anything multi-step. Hallucination and imprecision below the 9B line are not edge cases — they are the norm. Serving those answers doesn't democratize intelligence; it just ships wrong answers with a friendly tone.

So we drew a line, calibrated by a quality score rather than raw parameter count (so a Mixture-of-Experts 35B-A3B is judged on what it delivers, not its active params). The viable floor is 9B (quality score 75); 7B (65) is the extreme we tolerate; below that, the network will not use the model as a final answerer.

Graceful, never a hard failure. When no model at or above the floor is free, the strongest available worker answers anyway — we never return a 503. The floor is a preference for quality, not a wall.

Your small machine is not wasted

A sub-9B contributor is not a second-class citizen. Smaller models do real, paid work every minute:

RoleWhat it does
Trivial promptsShort, low-stakes requests are served directly by smaller models — fast and cheap.
Classification & routingDeciding which tier a prompt needs is itself useful inference a small model does well.
Checker / reviewSmaller models help verify and post-process larger models' drafts.

All of this earns network credits under the same reward split. Every atom contributes; no contribution is wasted. We are honest about which atom should write the final answer to a hard question — that's all.

Reciprocity: contribution and consumption

The network runs on a simple balance: it is neither commercial nor a free buffet, but a distributed budget where each participant finds a measurable interest without anyone silently footing the bill. In that spirit, consuming the community's compute through the API is tied to actively contributing compute back — running a capable worker connected to a pool. No participation, no free ride. (This reciprocity rule is rolling out in phases; see the release notes.)

In short: participate with anything; we serve answers worth trusting. Heterogeneity for the network, a floor for the answer.
